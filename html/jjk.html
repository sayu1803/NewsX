Popular One Piece, Jujutsu Kaisen Animator Says He'll Never Work for MAPPA Again

Written By XYZ April 14, 2021

Anime

Vincent Chansard's recent statement has sent shockwaves through the anime community, further amplifying concerns about the tumultuous state of MAPPA, the renowned anime studio. As one of the latest animators to voice grievances against the studio, Chansard's declaration adds to a growing chorus of discontent within the industry. Chansard's decision to speak out comes at a critical juncture, following the conclusion of the highly anticipated and visually stunning Luffy vs. Kaido showdown in One Piece, which he had been diligently working on. The animator's extended silence on social media, particularly X (formerly Twitter), for two months since the culmination of this epic battle had left fans and industry insiders speculating about the reasons behind his absence. Breaking his silence on November 16, 2023, Chansard revealed a troubling sentiment, stating, "2 years ago I said I would never work for MAPPA again." This revelation raises questions about the working conditions or other issues within MAPPA that may have prompted such a strong stance from a talented animator. As the anime community anxiously awaits further details, Chansard's disclosure adds fuel to existing concerns about MAPPA's internal challenges. The studio, known for its exceptional animation quality, is now under increasing scrutiny, and fans are left wondering about the potential ramifications for ongoing and future projects. The situation surrounding MAPPA continues to unfold, casting a shadow over the once-celebrated studio and prompting industry-wide reflections on the welfare of anime creators.
